"The doctors said the paralysis was final. But the Word of the Lord had the last decree."

I lived in the silence of a wheelchair for twenty-two years. My legs were as dead as the winter branches of a fallen tree. Specialists across three continents reviewed my charts, each shaking their head with the same clinical finality. 'Nerve damage of this magnitude,' they would say, 'is irreversible.'

Then came the day of the visitation. As the man of God declared the healing of crippled bones, I felt a heat unlike any sun. It started in my marrow—a violent, holy restoration. Before I knew I was moving, I was standing. Before I could think, I was running.
